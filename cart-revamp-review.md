# Cart Revamp Case Study Review

## Context
Reviewing this case study as a Product Design Lead evaluating a candidate for a Senior Product Designer role. The review assesses storytelling, process demonstration, business impact, and senior-level competencies.

---

## Overall Assessment: **Strong Candidate — Would Interview**

This case study demonstrates senior-level thinking with clear research-to-delivery narrative, cross-functional collaboration, and honest reflection. A few gaps prevent it from being exceptional.

---

## Strengths

### 1. Research-Grounded Problem Definition
- Opens with external authority (Baymard) to frame industry context
- Synthesizes multiple research sources: usability testing (38% comprehension stat), interviews, journey mapping, experiments
- User quotes are specific and emotionally resonant ("It feels like they are trying to trick me")
- The "What users told us" section is excellent — shows pattern recognition across research methods

### 2. Clear Design Principles → Decisions Link
- Three principles (Assurance, Modification, Price clarity) directly trace to user pain points
- Decisions later in the case study reference back to these principles
- Shows strategic thinking, not just execution

### 3. Honest About Constraints and Trade-offs
- "What didn't make the final cut" section shows mature prioritization thinking
- Payment method descoping decision is particularly strong — demonstrates data-informed judgment
- "Forks in the road" section shows awareness of organizational complexity

### 4. Experimental Rigor
- 2x2 experiment design to isolate cart changes from UI2 rebrand shows methodological sophistication
- Clear about statistical significance (or lack thereof) while still justifying rollout decision
- Includes both quantitative metrics and behavioral signals

### 5. Scope and Impact
- Global rollout across 8 brands, 3 platforms
- Led end-to-end from discovery through deployment
- Design system contribution (brand tokens, first multi-brand feature)

---

## Areas for Improvement

### 1. Missing: The Actual Design Decisions
**Critical gap.** The case study explains *what* shipped but not *why specific design choices were made*. Questions I'd want answered:
- Why two-column layout vs. other approaches considered?
- How did you decide on the information hierarchy?
- What iterations happened between vision and final?
- Why these specific label treatments for add-on pairing?

**Recommendation:** Add a section showing 2-3 key design decisions with alternatives considered and rationale.

### 2. Weak Before/After Comparison
The Before → Vision → Iteration image is hard to parse. I can't clearly see:
- What changed
- Why it's better
- How the changes map to the principles

**Recommendation:** Either annotate the comparison more clearly OR break it into focused before/after pairs for specific improvements (e.g., "How we clarified add-on context").

### 3. Design Delivery Section Missing
The case study mentions "Design Delivery" work (design system composite, UX writer collaboration, documentation, localization) but doesn't show it. This is valuable senior-level work that differentiates execution quality.

**Recommendation:** Show one artifact — a component spec, a decision tree, or a documentation sample.

### 4. "My Role" is Passive
The role description lists activities but doesn't differentiate contribution:
- "I led usability testing" — but what did you *find* and *decide*?
- "collaborated with a UX research partner" — what was *your* specific contribution vs. theirs?

**Recommendation:** Be more specific about decisions you personally drove vs. participated in.

### 5. Results Could Be Stronger
The engagement metrics are good (11.6% delivery drawer, +3.6% savings accordion), but:
- No conversion funnel impact shown
- No customer satisfaction or qualitative feedback post-launch
- "Directionally positive but not significant" is honest but undersells

**Recommendation:** If available, add one qualitative signal (support tickets down, NPS comment, etc.) to complement behavioral data.

---

## Interview Questions I'd Ask

1. **Design rationale:** "Walk me through one specific design decision where you considered multiple approaches. What did you decide and why?"

2. **Stakeholder management:** "You mentioned negotiating with teams who wanted cart real estate. Tell me about a specific negotiation — how did you say no?"

3. **Research application:** "The 38% comprehension stat is striking. How did that specific finding change your design approach?"

4. **Reflection:** "You said you'd push harder on price clarity. If you could go back, what specifically would you do differently in the first week of the project?"

5. **Systems thinking:** "How did working on the cart influence your thinking about the broader checkout experience?"

## Summary

| Criteria | Rating | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Problem definition | Strong | Research-grounded, well-synthesized |
| Process demonstration | Moderate | Missing design exploration/rationale |
| Business impact | Moderate | Good engagement metrics, financial impact unclear |
