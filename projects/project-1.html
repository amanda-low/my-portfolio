Healthcare Service Redesign

Reimagining the patient journey from appointment booking to follow-up care for a regional healthcare network.

Role Lead Service Designer
Timeline 6 months
Team 4 designers, 2 researchers
Methods Service Blueprinting, Journey Mapping, Co-design

Overview

The Challenge

A regional healthcare network was experiencing high patient drop-off rates and consistently poor satisfaction scores. Patients reported feeling lost in the system, with an average wait time of 45 minutes and no visibility into their care journey.

My Contribution

As the lead service designer, I owned the end-to-end service design process. I facilitated stakeholder workshops, led research synthesis, created the service blueprint, and worked directly with implementation teams to ensure design intent carried through to execution.

Research & Discovery

We conducted 24 patient interviews, shadowed 12 clinical staff members, and analyzed 6 months of operational data. Key insights emerged:

  • Fragmented touchpoints: Patients interacted with an average of 7 different systems/staff members for a single appointment
  • Information gaps: 68% of patients arrived unprepared due to unclear pre-visit instructions
  • Staff workarounds: Clinical staff had developed unofficial processes that weren't supported by existing tools

Research synthesis: organizing findings into themes and opportunity areas

Process

Service Blueprint

I created a comprehensive service blueprint mapping the current state across all touchpoints, frontstage interactions, and backstage processes. This revealed critical failure points where the patient experience broke down.

Current-state service blueprint highlighting pain points and opportunities

Co-Design Workshops

I facilitated three co-design sessions with patients, clinical staff, and administrators. By bringing these groups together, we generated solutions that addressed real constraints while meeting patient needs.

Solution

The redesigned service introduced three key interventions:

1. Unified Pre-Visit Experience

A single, clear communication flow that provides patients with everything they need before their visit—directions, preparation instructions, and what to expect.

2. Real-Time Status Updates

Digital signage and SMS updates that give patients visibility into wait times and their position in the queue.

3. Care Coordinator Role

A new staff role that serves as a single point of contact for patients navigating complex care journeys.

Future-state journey showing streamlined touchpoints and improved communication

Outcome

Six months after implementation, the pilot locations showed significant improvements:

  • 40% reduction in average wait times (45 min → 27 min)
  • 23-point increase in patient satisfaction scores
  • 35% decrease in no-show rates due to improved pre-visit communication
  • 92% staff adoption of new processes within the first month

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of designing for all actors in a service ecosystem, not just end users. The staff workarounds we discovered became some of our most valuable design inputs—they represented real-world solutions that had evolved organically.

If I could do it again, I would have involved IT stakeholders earlier in the process. Some of our initial concepts had to be significantly revised due to technical constraints that could have been identified sooner.
